<p><p>Geothermal electric power generation involves extracting heat from the earth's interior to produce electricity. Dry steam stations use steam directly from underground to power turbines. Flash steam power stations convert high-pressure hot water into steam to generate electricity. Binary cycle stations use lower temperature water or brine to heat a secondary fluid with a lower boiling point, which then powers a turbine. Each of these market types harnesses geothermal energy in different ways to produce electricity, contributing to the overall expansion of the geothermal electric power generation market.</p></p>
